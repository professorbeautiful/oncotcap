The purpose of OncoTCap

 

"Oncology Thinking Cap" (OncoTCap) is a unique computer software environment presenting a browser environment through which cancer researchers can "travel" to investigate knowledge, concepts, and working simulation models without need of a computer scientist for a guide. OncoTCap is a platform-independent computer program for integrating cancer knowledge acquisition with model-building, to facilitate a wide array of applications in research and education. Some examples are:

• To facilitate the work of people with diverse skills and goals, so that individuals doing cancer modeling can work together effectively within teams. (The Tri-flow)
• To use natural scientific language to represent and re-use biological and clinical ideas. (Statement Templates, Query Templates)
• To incorporate an expanding set of knowledge acquisition helpers (EDGAR; caBIO; InsightLink).
• To facilitate use of other modeling engines besides the native simulator. (Projects of the CViT Integrated Cancer Biology program; NetLogo; many others)
• To facilitate combining models of different sub-processes, from different teams and even using different engines. (Sophisticated model representation format and pre-processor.)
• To foster creative thinking by “iconoclast support”, that is by making the catalog of modeling statements rich enough to remove restrictions on hypotheses that might be entertained. (Statement Templates)
• To automate self-documentation tracing the decisions and information sources feeding into model-building.
• To support modelers in acquiring data and sharing results.
